GETTING STARTED

BIA School is focused mainly on building and preparing our learners to be able to gain grounds in the world that awaits them. To be able to standout and make a difference. The senior classes are prepared to partake in external exams and we have recorded an outstanding success in these few years. As an international school, we are happy to provide our learners with the highest possible standard of education, enabling them to compete in an increasingly competitive global environment.

The need to maintain this standard demand not just in teaching methods which are appropriate to the needs of our learners but that those learners, in turn, are able to demonstrate that they have the potential or ability to achieve the levels we require. Admission is not based on ethnic, religion or creed. It's all based on merits. Admission is at school discretion and the schol's decision is final. Only if a students fails to show basic understanding, or shows a lack of potential will they be refused admission.

OUR CURRICULUM

BIA Group of schools offers a broad curriculum and provides learners with opportunities to study range of subjects and to acquire a variety of important practical and academic skills. We ensure that students follow a broad study programme, carefully designed to meet the requirement of the National Curriculum. Through our programmes, we prepare our students for sucessful completion of their secondary education passing WASSCE, NECO and any other external exams which can be encountered.

EXTRA-CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES

We recognise academic excellence alone is not enough, which is why we invest also in extra curricular activities that help our students to develop and stimulate creativities, build confidence and self-esteem, learn life skills and create social awareness.

To enrich all of our student's education and to develop team building, fitness and friendship with healthy competition, clubs are organized, sport activities such as football, Table Tennis, Board games, Dance and Drama, and Computer creatives.

Taking tour and excursion is also part of the extra curricular activities that we get our students involved in.

ASSESSMENT

We make use of many differenct methods of assessment, some of which include

  1. Class Assessment - Short test immediately after a topic comes handy in testing the understanding of the students
  2. Tutor Assessment - This is carried out at interval and at tutor descretion during the course to assess and evaluate the progress and developed skills of the students
  3. Formal Examination - This is done at the end of each term to get the overall evaluation of the students progress

School examination results are clearly indicated on the students' reports, so parents are fully aware of their child's progress. We conduct parent and teacher meeting at regular intervals when necessary during the school year to discuss issues partaining to the success of the students academically and morally.

PARENTS AS PARTNERS

The National Education Board also affirm that parents are child's primary educators and therefore encourage parents to contribute to their child's learning. Parents partnership are key to a successful experience for their children and for them to gain the most out of their early education and reach expected levels of development. This responsibility usually falls to the teacher to ensure an effective relationship is built and share information at the beginning and end of the day, reason why the open day is important so cogent issues can be discussed.

Importance of Parents Partnership

  • Parents know and undertsand their children the best
  • To share information about new levels of development, any concerns and any new likes or dislikes
  • To keep upto date with whats is happening inside and outside the school
  • Parents can feel secured that their children is in safe hands
  • Improves practice and outcomes for the children, ensuring every child has their full individual needs met.
